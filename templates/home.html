Prominent Indian Architecures

Ancient Architecture

Ancient Indian architecture is the architecture of the Indian subcontinent from the Indian Bronze Age to around 800 CE. It includes buddhist monastries, stupas,stone architectures, rock-cut architecture. Nagara and Dravidian architectural styles are also part of ancient architecture.

British Architecture

A revivalist architectural style was mostly used by British architects in India in the later 19th century, especially in public and government buildings in the British Raj, and the palaces of rulers of the princely states. It drew stylistic and decorative elements from native Indo-Islamic architecture, especially Mughal architecture.

Indo-Islamic

It is the architecture of the Indian subcontinent produced by and for Islamic patrons and purposes. It's development began in earnest with the establishment of Delhi as the capital of the Ghurid dynasty in 1193.

Maratha Architecture

Maratha temples, built between the mid-seventeenth and early nineteenth centuries in Maharashtra (Western India), were influenced by the preceding traditions of Hindu temple architecture of the Yadavas and the religious and secular buildings of Deccani Sultanates and Mughals.

Sikh Architecture

It was developed under Sikh Empire during 18th and 19th century in the Punjab region. The architecture was heavily influenced by Mughal and Islamic styles.
